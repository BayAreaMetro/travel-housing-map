Maps

Welcome to maps.onebayarea.org. If you’ve landed here, it’s likely you’re already engaged in the onebayarea.com innovative regional planning effort addressing climate change, sustainable growth and development, transportation needs, and protection of our air and water. If you’re not involved in this unprecedented, collaborative, multi-agency and multi-jurisdiction regional planning challenge, we strongly encourage you to learn more.

Whereas regional planning focuses on the overall health of the entire region, this interactive map site is all about bringing to life the rich regional planning data that’s important to your life. In other words, these interactive maps are designed to help translate the emerging vision of the OneBayArea plan into personalized dynamic stories of what this vision means to your health, happiness and quality of life.

The first map in this series, the Travel Time and Housing Prices map, helps show the relationship between travel time, using different modes of transit, and housing prices in the bay area.

Why is this important to you? Well, the quality of our lives is deeply connected to where we live, work, play and learn, and how we move between these places. So, for example, if you work in Berkeley and would like to know where you might find a house for between $250,000 and $400,000, this map will show you where. Furthermore, if you want to limit your commute to only 30 minutes by car, transit, bike or walking, you can see how these transportation preferences and limits affect your ability to find an affordable place to live that’s close to where you spend your time working, playing or learning.

We hope you enjoy your exploration of this map and data and find it useful to your life story—both the opportunities and challenges you face living in the bay area.
